Strengths (3 points) The paper presents the winning solution of last year KDD Cup.

Multi-stage ensemble and the comprehensive feature engineering.
Weaknesses (3 points)
Lack of explanation on the feature list. The authors need to explain why the features are important. Most of the features are not explained.

This paper also needs to show the difference between this approach and previous approaches in churn detection.
Detailed Comments The technical values of the solution to detect drop out in MOOC are high. However, the paper needs many revisions. It looks like the authors assume readers are familiar with the context of the KDD Cup 2015. For example, The paper needs to define 'drop out' in this context. To make the paper self sufficient, the authors need to explain the raw data: the structure and may be some examples.

The features listed in the paper needs to be explained in more detailed, such as the rationale/motivation and how to interpret the values. As an example, it is difficult to understand some features in Section 2.6-2.9.

This paper also needs to show the difference between this approach and previous approaches in churn detection.

Detailed Comments This paper presents an analytics pipeline from feature engineering to single models training and then to multi-stage ensembles. It describes how this method was used for MOOC dropout prediction in KDD Cup 15. Feature engineering depends on both hand crafted features and auto encoding features.

-The motivation behind the proposed approach is not clear. The introduction should focus on the motivation, problem, solution … and not on the KDD Cup.
-In introduction, it is necessary to provide a paragraph as a clear summary of your proposed solution.
-Organization and presentation need improvement. Information about datasets and extracted features can be better presented in tables.
-Careful polishing is necessary. There are too many small English errors in the text that need correction. Some sentences are not clear.
- the impact of stage-2 and stage-3 is not clear
